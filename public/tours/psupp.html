SECTION 7 — Ignitionp. 47

The ignition system uses a transistorized magneto-type coil mounted on the engine block. No-spark conditions are most often caused by a short in the kill-switch wire (yellow), a failed coil, or a fouled spark plug.

Procedure for diagnosis:

1. Disconnect the kill-switch wire at the coil terminal. Test for spark.

2. If spark returns, the kill switch or wire is at fault.
